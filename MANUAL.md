# Clam Jumper

**Pick your reef hunter, crank clams open, and out-pearl the rival before the eel finds you.**

## The story

Somewhere on a moonlit reef, clams full of pearls have settled into the coral —
and word gets around fast underwater. You are one of the reef's real clam
specialists: a sea star, an octopus, or a ray. So is your rival. Whoever pries
more pearls owns the reef... and yes, if you leave a clam half-open, the rival
*will* jump your claim.

## Controls

- **D-pad** — move tile to tile around the reef (one direction at a time)
- **Crank** — pry open the clam you're standing on; a progress ring fills and
  the shell gapes wider the further you crank
- **A** — your species' signature ability (see below); also confirms menus
- **A (hold)** — the ray keeps gliding for as long as you hold it

## How to play

Each reef is a coral maze scattered with closed clams, each holding one pearl.
Stand on a clam and crank until the ring completes — the pearl is yours
(100 points). Step off early and the progress slowly bleeds back out.

Meanwhile a **rival** hunts the same clams, prying them open on its own. Clear
every clam to finish the reef; whoever took more pearls wins the reef bonus
(ties go to you). Then it's down to the next, deeper reef: more clams, more
eels, a faster rival.

### Choose your hunter

All three share the same chassis — walk, crank, ability — but play very
differently:

- **Sea Star — the grip.** Slowest mover, but clams give up easiest (shortest
  crank of the three), progress barely bleeds when you step off, and a rival
  bump only *halves* your progress instead of erasing it. **A clamps down**
  for a moment: nothing can bump you and eels slide harmlessly past — but you
  can't move while clamped. The catch: the sea star cannot hop at all. Coral
  and eels must be routed around or clamped through.
- **Octopus — the jet.** Fastest mover with a standard crank, but pry progress
  drains fast the moment you wander off — commit or lose it. **A jet-dashes
  3 tiles** in your facing direction, sailing over coral and eels (shorter if
  the far tiles are blocked), and leaves an **ink puff** on the tile you left.
  A rival that wades into your ink is stunned; eels ignore ink — they hunt by
  smell.
- **Ray — the glide.** Smooth and quick. **Hold A to glide** over coral, eels,
  everything, for up to 4 tiles — you land where you release (the ray politely
  avoids setting down on an eel if it can land just beyond instead). Landing
  square on a clam is a **wing-slam** that blasts it part-open for free. The
  cooldown scales with distance flown, so short hops recover almost instantly.

Your last pick is remembered as the default next game.

### The rival

Each reef the rival spawns as a random species *other than yours*, and hunts
like it:

- A **rival octopus** jet-dashes straight at your contested clam and drops ink
  that stuns *you* if you step in it.
- A **rival sea star** is slow and never chases you — but it pries clams open
  far faster than the other rivals. It farms; out-run it.
- A **rival ray** covers corridors in gliding two-tile hops.

If you're partway into a clam (past about a quarter), the rival will come and
**bump you off it**: you lose your progress (sea star: half), get stunned a
beat, and get shoved a tile away. A clamped sea star can't be bumped at all —
the rival gives up and goes farming instead. The rival gets a little faster
with every reef.

### The moray eel

A moray eel patrols the open corridors, mostly holding its heading. Touch it
and you lose a life. You are safe mid-dash, mid-glide, mid-hop (airborne =
untouchable), while clamped, and for a couple of seconds after respawning.
Deeper reefs add more eels, and they slither faster.

### Pearl Rain (bonus round)

After every second reef, pearls drift down the water column and you scoot
along the seabed catching them — 75 points each, no hazards, pure profit,
nine seconds on the clock.

## Scoring, lives, progression

| Event | Points |
|---|---|
| Pearl pried | 100 |
| Reef cleared | 250 |
| Reef won (you ≥ rival) | 1000 |
| Bonus pearl caught | 75 |

- Start with **3 lives**, maximum 5. **Extra life every 10,000 points.**
- Each reef adds one clam (up to 11), thickens the coral, speeds the rival,
  and every other reef adds an eel.
- High score (and your species pick) are saved on the device.

## Hazards & pickups at a glance

- **Clam** — holds one pearl; crank to open. Progress decays if you leave it.
- **Rival** — races you for pearls; bumps you off contested clams.
- **Ink puff** (octopus dash) — lingers a couple of seconds; stuns the *other*
  side that steps in. Eels are immune.
- **Moray eel** — lethal on touch; dodge, out-hop, or clamp through it.

## Tips

- **Ties count as a win.** You take the 1000-point reef bonus whenever your
  pearls at least match the rival's — securing an even split is worth a fight.
- **The rival only contests clams past ~25% pried.** Early cranking is safe;
  once the ring passes a quarter, expect company. Finish fast or be ready.
- **Sea star:** you barely lose anything to bumps or decay, so don't panic —
  claw progress back at your own pace. Save the clamp for eels arriving while
  you crank; if clamp is cooling, just walk away (it's only a 4-second wait).
- **Octopus:** with the fastest decay in the game, never start a clam you
  can't finish. Dash *through* trouble — the ink you leave behind turns your
  escape into an ambush on a chasing rival.
- **Ray:** the wing-slam is a quarter of a clam for free — glide onto clams
  instead of walking, and keep glides short: one-tile hops cost only a
  0.3-second cooldown but still give you i-frames over an eel.
- **Against a rival sea star, spread out.** It pries fast but crawls and never
  chases — race it to the far clams and let it grind the near ones.
